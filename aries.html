User Research on the Alaska ARIES Systems Support project

Serving as Design Lead throughout a partnership between RedMane and Alaska's Department of Health and Human Services (DHSS) to support the State's Medicaid eligibility system.

In 2021 Redmane engaged the State of Alaska Department of Health and Human services to provide systems support to the state's Medicaid Eligibility System, ARIES. Our team of 11 RedManers integrates with Alaska's ARIES dev team, and on our team we have two designers: myself, the design lead, as well as another UX designer I was asked to mentor until they would one day take on the role of design lead. At the outset of the project I authored a user-centered design plan that documented the high expectations of and strict requirements for UX research on our project, which included integrating UX research into our two week Agile sprint cadence. On the heels of authoring this document, my UX team member and myself began seeking UX opportunities.

The first research project we completed was an affinity wall analysis. We conducted user interviews, extracted insights, then arranged the insights into an affinity wall, and conveyed our findings in a report. Our second research project involved conducting a usability heuristics analysis on the application. We tested the application against Nielsen’s 10 heuristics, analyzed the results, and documented an organized, prioritized list of UX improvement opportunities to act on in future sprints.
